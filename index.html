The Legend

Born From One Man's Obsession

It all started with a pressure cooker, a roadside restaurant in Corbin, Kentucky, and a man named Harland Sanders who refused to give up. After decades of perfecting his recipe, Colonel Sanders created something the world had never tasted before — impossibly crispy, juicy, perfectly seasoned fried chicken.

At the age of 65, with his first Social Security cheque and an unshakeable belief in his recipe, he franchised his first restaurant. Today, KFC serves millions of customers every single day across more than 150 countries. Not bad for a colonel who never gave up.

The secret? Eleven herbs and spices blended into the world's most recognisable coating — a recipe so precious it's kept under lock and key, split between two separate companies so no one person knows the full formula.

Creamy, crunchy, and refreshing. KFC's coleslaw is the perfect cooling contrast to the bold spices of the chicken. Made fresh daily with shredded cabbage and a legendary dressing. Don't sleep on it.

Through The Years

The KFC Story

1930
Harland Sanders opens a small service station in Corbin, Kentucky. He begins cooking for hungry travellers — his fried chicken quickly becomes the main attraction. Word spreads fast.
1940
The Colonel perfects his secret blend of 11 herbs and spices and introduces the pressure cooker method — dramatically cutting cooking time while sealing in all the juicy flavour that makes KFC unmistakable.
1952
The first KFC franchise opens in Salt Lake City, Utah. Sanders drives across the country in his car, cooking chicken for restaurant owners and asking for a small royalty on every piece sold. The empire begins.
1964
At 73 years old, Sanders sells the company for $2 million. By this point there are already 600 KFC locations across the United States and Canada. He stays on as brand ambassador and global ambassador for life.
1971
KFC goes international in a massive way — over 3,500 outlets in 48 countries. The brand becomes a global phenomenon, adapting its menu to local tastes while keeping the original recipe at its heart.
2023
KFC operates over 27,000 restaurants in more than 150 countries. From the UK's beloved Zinger Tower Burger to Japan's Christmas tradition, KFC has woven itself into the fabric of global food culture forever.
Top Secret

The 11 Herbs
& Spices

The exact recipe is one of the most closely guarded trade secrets in history. Kept in a vault at KFC's Louisville headquarters, handwritten by the Colonel himself. Here's what food scientists and journalists believe goes into the mix...

Salt
The foundation. Draws moisture out, seasons deep into the meat. Non-negotiable.
White Pepper
Sharper and more complex than black pepper. Adds heat without bitterness.
Garlic Salt
That unmistakable savoury depth. Garlic without the rawness.
Paprika
Gives the coating its beautiful golden-red colour and subtle sweetness.
Celery Salt
Adds an herby, slightly bitter note that rounds out the flavour profile.
Black Pepper
Classic warmth and bite. Works alongside white pepper for complexity.
Dried Mustard
A tangy kick that brings everything together without tasting like mustard.
Ginger
Warm, slightly floral spice that lifts the whole blend beautifully.
Basil
A subtle herby note that adds freshness and complexity to the coating.
Oregano
Earthy, aromatic — brings the Mediterranean warmth to the spice blend.
Marjoram
The mystery herb. Floral and sweet, the Colonel's secret finishing touch.

🔬

The Science of the Crunch

KFC uses a pressure cooker rather than a deep fryer — a method the Colonel invented himself. The pressurised environment forces steam into the meat while sealing the coating, creating that legendary combination of crackling crust and impossibly moist chicken underneath. You simply can't replicate it at home.

🧬

Flavour Memory

Food scientists call it "sensory nostalgia." The combination of fat, salt, and umami in KFC's coating triggers deep pleasure centres in the brain while the smell alone — those 11 herbs and spices hitting hot oil — can transport you instantly back to your childhood. KFC doesn't just feed you; it feels like a memory.

🌍

A Truly Global Icon

In Japan, KFC has become the traditional Christmas dinner — over 3.6 million families order it on Christmas Eve. In China, it's the most popular Western restaurant brand. In the UK, it's the go-to post-night-out ritual. KFC has achieved something remarkable: a single restaurant that means something deeply personal to billions of different people.
